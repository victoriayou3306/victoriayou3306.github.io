Final Project for Vanderbilt University's ME 4271: Introduction to Robotics, implementing Resolved Rates for Mecademic's MECA500. I took this course during my senior year of high school, so I didn't have swipe access to the Vanderbilt robotics lab. Because of this, my partner Seth focused on the GUI/physical implementation while worked on the simulation and resolved rates.

This was my first time working on Serial and Industrial robots, and I was able to learn a lot. It made the 40 minute commute to class very worth it. The simulation and resolved rates was pretty straightforward, especially having some experience with the Continuum project. The biggest challenge was collision and singularity avoidance. While the physical arm had encoders that would detect crashes and emergency stop, the stls in simulation did not have the same. The slideshow gives some video examples, as well as Seth's GUI implementation + a pick & place demo.
